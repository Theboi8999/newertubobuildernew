--- WHAT IS THE BIGGEST QUALITY GAP? ---

MATERIAL VARIETY — score 50/100, identified as a bottleneck.

  390 out of 396 parts (98.5%) use material="smoothplastic".
  Only 6 parts use concrete (the ground slab and floor slabs).

  Root cause: The research result correctly returns:
    wallMaterial: "limestone"
    roofMaterial: "slate"
    groundMaterial: "pavement"
    columnMaterial: "concrete"
    floorBandMaterial: "smoothplastic"

  BUT neither VM_MAP (blueprint-compiler.ts) nor VM_MAP (rbxmx.ts)
  maps "limestone" or "slate" to any Roblox material — both return
  "smoothplastic" as the fallback. The material data flows in correctly
  through the ResearchResult and StyleDNA, but is silently discarded
  at the part generation level. The VM_MAP needs:
    limestone → SmoothPlastic (or brick — closest visual)
    slate     → concrete (or smoothplastic with dark color)
    pavement  → concrete

  Current effect: every wall, roof, column, band, and window surround
  is smoothplastic. A brick Roblox material on the walls and concrete
  on the ground/columns would add significant visual fidelity for zero
  geometry cost.

  Secondary gaps (from quality check):
    • Street furniture: score 30 (none — by design when options.scenery
      not set; this is expected behavior, not a bug)
    • Room overlap: score 60 (2 BSP room pairs collide — see fix #4)


--- TOP 5 SPECIFIC THINGS TO FIX ---

FIX 1: analysePrompt contaminating buildingType with "floors"
  EVIDENCE: buildingType extracted = "singapore_peranakan_shophouse_floors"
            The word "floors" from "4 floors" was absorbed into the
            building type name.
  IMPACT: In production, researchBuildingType() searches for
          "singapore_peranakan_shophouse_floors" — a string no
          database or AI knows. The isPeranakan override saves this
          in local testing, but any production logs or caching will
          store the wrong key.
  FIX: In lib/prompt-intelligence.ts, strip floor count words
       ("floor", "floors", "storey", "storeys", "story") from the
       extracted buildingType before returning it.

FIX 2: Material pipeline is broken — VM_MAP drops all specialist materials
  EVIDENCE: wallMaterial="limestone" → smoothplastic (VM_MAP fallback)
            roofMaterial="slate" → smoothplastic (VM_MAP fallback)
            groundMaterial="pavement" → concrete (VM_MAP has "pavement")
            columnMaterial="concrete" → concrete (correct)
  IMPACT: 390/396 parts are smoothplastic. Building looks flat/plastic
          regardless of architectural style.
  FIX: Add to VM_MAP in blueprint-compiler.ts and rbxmx.ts:
         limestone: 'brick', render: 'smoothplastic', stucco: 'smoothplastic',
         tile: 'smoothplastic', terracotta: 'brick', slate: 'concrete'
       Also ensure StyleDNA.wallMaterial/roofMaterial actually passes
       through to the mkWall/generateRoof part calls (currently not used).

FIX 3: floorHeight is hardcoded in buildExterior — r.floorHeight ignored
  EVIDENCE: buildExterior line 120: "const fh = 12" — hardcoded.
            r.floorHeight is never read in exterior generation.
            quality-checker measures 55 studs vs expected 48.
  IMPACT: floorHeight field in ResearchResult is dead weight for
          exterior geometry. Tall buildings (mosques at fh=14,
          warehouses at fh=14) all get 12-stud floors instead.
  FIX: Change buildExterior to:
         const fh = Math.max(8, Math.min(18, r.floorHeight || 12))
       This uses the researched value while clamping to safe bounds.

FIX 4: BSP room placer produces overlapping rooms (2 collisions)
  EVIDENCE: quality-checker reports "2 overlapping room pair(s)"
            Room layout: Reception @ x=9.5 z=18.5 (13w × 12d)
                         Office @ x=20.0 z=8.0 (14w × 10d)
                         Storage @ x=9.5 z=6.5 (10w × 7d)
  IMPACT: Score docked to 60/100 for Room Overlap check.
          In 3D, overlapping rooms produce intersecting geometry
          that looks broken when viewed from above or with walls.
  FIX: In lib/room-placer.ts, after BSP placement, run a
       post-process separation pass: for each overlapping pair,
       translate the smaller room away from the larger until
       bounding boxes no longer intersect (push along the shortest
       axis). A simple greedy loop of 10 iterations is sufficient.

FIX 5: Style library floorCount override too conservative
  EVIDENCE: applyStyleDefaults only overrides floorCount if
            r.floorCount <= 1. So if the AI returns floorCount=2
            for a shophouse (common — AI underestimates Asian
            buildings), the library's floorCount=3 is skipped.
            Only the forced isPeranakan override (which hard-sets 4)
            saves this case. Other building types with no forced
            override (e.g., Gothic, baroque) will be stuck at
            whatever undercount the AI returns.
  IMPACT: A "Gothic cathedral 1 floor" would get floorCount=4 from
          the style library correctly (since 1 <= 1), but a
          "Gothic cathedral 2 floors" would be stuck at 2 despite
          the library saying 4. The forced-override safety net only
          exists for Peranakan/Victorian.
  FIX: Change applyStyleDefaults to use the style library floor count
       as a MINIMUM, not a replacement:
         floorCount: Math.max(r.floorCount, def.floorCount)
       This ensures the style library acts as a floor (no pun
       intended) rather than an override-only value.
